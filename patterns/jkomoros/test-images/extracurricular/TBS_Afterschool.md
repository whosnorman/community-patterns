Registration and Payment
 All students at TBS may register for Enrichment classes. Additionally, 
Students from other schools may register for Enrichment classes if they are signed up for The Depot. 
Tinker Coop programs are open to the entire community whether or not they are TBS or The Depot students. 
Enrichment classes are taught by external providers and incur fees in addition to The Depot/ExDay fees. 
Complete one form for each student by Friday, January 9th. Timely registration is important for instructors who need to know if they have enough enrollment and materials, and for overall program administration.
Class registration is on a first-come, first-served basis: students who register after the max has been reached will be added to a waitlist. For the competitive FIRST Robotics teams, a form does not guarantee enrollment, and a selection process may be involved. 
TBS will notify parenting adults whose emails were included during registration if maximum capacity has been reached and/or if enrollment is insufficient to meet the minimum level needed.
Registration closes after the first class meeting and there are no refunds after the second class meeting.
TBS students who are not enrolled in ExDay and participate in Enrichment classes that start more than 15 minutes after their school day ends, or need ExDay supervision between Enrichment classes for more than 15 minutes will be charged a reduced ExDay rate for the Fall session as follows: $130/session Mondays/Fridays, $150/session Tuesdays/Wednesdays, $140/session Thursdays. 
Parenting adults will be billed through Smart Tuition. Please note that any payments for Tinker‚ÄØ Coop programs will be processed separately and will not be billed through, nor appear on your Smart Tuition statement.

Class Descriptions

Carpentry, Gr. K-3, 3-8 Kids‚Äô Carpentry is designed to teach kids the safe use of hand tools and general woodworking skills. Through a sequence of steps young carpenters gain the skill and competence to complete increasingly more complicated projects. From boats to bird feeders, doll furniture, clocks, games, airplanes, and many more, the kids have a wide range of projects to choose from, after first completing their sanding block and super-top. In addition to the fun, kids also gain quality experiences in measuring, applied math (including fractions), problem-solving, craftsmanship, and they are empowered by the hands-on experiences.

Chess, Gr. TK-8 The Chess Enrichment Program introduces students to the strategic world of chess in an engaging, developmentally appropriate way. It supports cognitive development, patience, problem-solving, and sportsmanship, while making learning fun through stories, challenges, and game-based learning. The program is ideal for students of all experience levels and encourages critical thinking, focus, and decision-making skills. TK students may require a parenting adult to support in class if needed.

CodeFu, Gr. K-5, 3-6 The Art of CodeFu is back and better than ever! From cyber security to video games to websites, our students learn the various projects that will give them invaluable coding/tech skills for their future. CodeFu: Mini Coding Dojo Using iPad/Asus tablet applications like Scratch Jr, Kodable, Light bot, and Bloxels coupled with hands-on board games that promote social problem-solving. CodeFu's Dojo teaches students the computational logic of Computer Science/coding as well as providing them with an introduction into a number of block-based coding languages.

Founded in 2015 in the San Francisco Bay Area, the Art of CodeFu was created with a unique combination of teachers with decades of experience in the classroom and top programmers in Silicon Valley integrating coding into subjects from PE to science. Our programs take coding to the next level by teaching our students about the yin & yang of technology & nature‚Äîthe importance of technology, but also its role in the balance with being active and in tune with the outside world.

Cooking, Gr. TK-3,  3-6 Ready, set, cook! This culinary adventure invites students to roll up their sleeves and dive into the delicious world of "Cooking With Friends." With a sprinkle of teamwork and a dash of fun, children will explore delightful sweet and savory recipes that nourish both body and mind. They'll develop essential kitchen skills like measuring, mixing, and knife safety, all while laughing and learning alongside their new friends. This series is a great way to boost confidence as kids follow directions and discover the joys of preparing healthy and delicious meals together! TK students may require a parenting adult to support in class if needed.

Dungeons & Dragons, Gr. 3-6, 4-8 D&D is a creative game of fantasy and high adventure that strengthens the imagination and collaborative skills of its players. Players take on the role of a fantastical character of their own devising and create sprawling adventures under the guidance of a dungeon master. Our dungeon masters bring years of professional theater training and experience into their games, calling on their creative backgrounds to create immersive environments that are fertile ground for imaginative play. We provide all materials required for play and welcome players of all experience levels. At the end of each session our dungeon masters will provide a written summary of the day‚Äôs journey so you can experience the adventure as it unfolds from week to week. Despite some stigma, Dungeons and Dragons isn‚Äôt an anti-social activity as it has been depicted in the past; it has evolved into an impactful method to promote socialization, critical thinking, and collaborative play that we believe all kids could benefit from.

Pickleball Gr. 2-8 Learn Pickleball, the fastest growing sport in America, with Higgins Tennis. Pickleball is easy to pick up and play right away! Upcycled Textile Art Gr. 3-8 Ignite a passion for creativity and sustainability in children with this fantastic textile art series! Young artists will dive into an extraordinary world where reusing and reinventing materials becomes an inspiring adventure. Through an exploration of collage-making, mixed media techniques, and up-cycling, kids will transform recycled textiles into unique pieces of art. They'll pick up basic hand-sewing skills, embrace the vibrant world of tie-dye, and even experiment with crochet and embroidery. Along the way, they'll explore the fundamentals of fashion design as they dream up new possibilities for old garments. It's a hands-on experience where creativity, sustainability, and self-expression come together in a joyful and eco-friendly celebration of textile art.

Vocal Training Gr. TK-8 Our Vocal Training program helps students discover the power of their voice while building confidence and self-expression. Each class balances technique with creativity, giving students opportunities to sing individually and as part of an ensemble. Vocal Training fosters Social Emotional Learning (SEL) by encouraging perseverance, resilience, and collaboration, while also strengthening communication skills. The program culminates in a showcase performance where students share their songs with peers and families, celebrating both technical growth and personal expression. TK students may require a parenting adult to support in class if needed.
